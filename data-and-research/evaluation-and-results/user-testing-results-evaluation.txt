USER TESTING RESULTS AND EVALUATION

User testing was conducted with two ESOL teachers and five ESOL students across two classes. 
Participants were asked to complete a series of tasks designed to evaluate teacher lesson 
management workflows, student lesson completion, and the AI-assisted feedback system. 
Both quantitative metrics and qualitative feedback were collected.

The testing aimed to evaluate usability, clarity of feedback, and overall usefulness 
to both teachers and students.

Teachers were able to complete all core tasks without assistance. The lesson creation workflow

This reduces a lot of their time spent on marking, which was one of the main motivations 
of the application.

Students were generally able to complete lessons without assistance. Instant feedback for 
multiple choice questions and fill-in-the-gap questions was well understood, while AI-generated 
feedback for writing and speaking questions required brief explanation for one participant. 

The retry feature was well received, with several students expressing motivation to improve 
their score. The progress tab was something most students said they would like to be improved 
as it did not have many motivating aspects to it.


TEACHER FEEDBACK

Teachers reported that:
- The application was very easy to use.
- Creating lessons and reviewing student writing was straightforward.
- The dashboard effectively centralises all parts of the application.
- The biggest advantage is the time saved compared to giving paper homework.

Suggested improvements:
- More metrics for each student.
- Ability to see which student has submitted work for feedback.
- Listening questions workflow


STUDENT FEEDBACK

Students reported that:
- The application workflow is easy to understand.
- The dashboard is clear and easy to use.
- Lessons are easy to complete and feedback is understandable.
- The practice page is helpful because they do not have to wait for teachers to upload lessons.
- The progress tab provides some motivation.

Suggested improvements:
- The progress tab should include better motivational metrics.

Bugs found:
-	First attempt and retry functionality not working as expected
-	Average score inconsistency across pages
-	Spelling question generation not as expected
-	Previous question button not working on practice page
-	Context menu appears below components when sidebar collapsed


OVERALL EVALUATION

Overall, user testing indicated that both teachers and students were able to use the system 
successfully with minimal assistance. The hybrid AI–teacher feedback model was perceived as 
particularly valuable by teachers because it reduces marking time while still allowing them 
to maintain oversight of student performance.

Teachers would like to see more detailed metrics for each student to better judge their progress, 
while students appreciated the AI marking system because it provides instant feedback and helps 
them understand how they performed.

Students also responded positively to the retry and practice functionality, as these features 
encourage continued improvement. However, they would prefer the progress page to include more 
motivational elements.


Limitations of Testing:
Despite positive results, the testing had several limitations. The participant group was relatively
small and consisted of only ESOL students of a few different languages. As a result, results may 
not generalise larger classroom environments or learners from different backgrounds. Additionally
testing sessions were conducted in a controlled environment rather than during real classroom usage
over an extended period. Also, this was tested quite quickly and not over a few weeks where users may
have found other things to improve on.

Improvements made after testing:
Several improvements were implemented following testing. An explanation was added to the practice page
to explain the two different practice types. Fixed the bugs that came noticed like how the average score
on one page was not matching on the other, or how the retry logic was not fully working etc.
