User Testing Plan:

Goals of the testing:

Primary goals:

- Validate that teachers can create, assign, and manage lessons/classes without confusion
- Validate students can complete lessons and understand feedback (instant and teacher approved AI feedback).
- Check that the review workflow is clear and not too time consuming as main motivation of this application is to reduce teacher workload and provide very fast feedback to students

Secondary goals:

- Practice feature usefulness: do students understand how to use it and do they think it helps
- The usefulness of the progress age, do they understand the metrics and does it help motivate them.
- Surface bugs/edge cases: auth, permissions, lesson states, retry rules etc.

Testing method:

Session format:

- 10 mins: introduction, consent, explain.
- 30-40 mins: Tasks
- 5-10 mins: Survery/Interview

Capturing feedback:

- Ask them to loink aloud when using the application
- Anything they like don’t like, anything they struggle with
- Note all of this down per user

Testing tasks:

Teacher tasks:

- Teacher registration
- Teacher adds students by email to relevant classes
- Teacher can view list of students in each class
- Teacher creates a couple of lessons and assigns to classes
- Teacher can save lesson as a draft and come back to it later
- Teacher can customise what type of questions they want to add
- Teacher cannot publish lesson unless all requirements met, 4 min questions, due date, lesson title etc.
- Teachers can edit an existing lesson
- Teachers can delete a draft lesson
- Filters/search in lessons data grid works
- Teachers can view student’s average marks and proficiency levels
- Sort and filtering works on student data grid
- Update a student’s proficiency level
- Teachers can open review tab and see completed submissions
- Teacher can edit AI feedback and score

Success criteria:

- Completion of tasks without help
- Teacher understands statuses
- Review process is quick and makes their life much easier rather than having to mark things manually all the time.

Student tasks:

- Student registration works
- Students can view the list of their lessons assigned to them by the teacher in to-do
- Students can view their past lessons in completed.
- Students can complete a lesson
- Students can start a lesson and continue later
- Students understand the feedback structure of the AI feedback.
- Students understand how the scoring works.
- Students understand that writing and speaking feedback is pending teacher approval
- After teacher approval students find and view feedback.
- Students can retry a completed lesson once
- Students can view both first attempt feedback and retry attempt feedback
- Students open the practice tab and start a common error practice lesson
- Students understand these questions are common errors for their L1.
- Students open the user-specific errors which show questions they have not got 100% on in their completed lessons.
- Students find average score/ proficiency level / streak in the progress tab.
- Students can interpret graphs and motivates them to improve.

Success criteria:

- Students can finish a lesson without getting stuck
- Students understand feedback timing and where to find it
- Practice is perceived as helpful, not confusing

Metrics to collect:
Quantitative:

- Task completion rate
- Time on task
- Error rate
- Number of help prompts needed
- Post task rating of ease (1-5)

Qualitative:

- Confusion points
- Feature value perception
- Trust in AI feedback
- Suggested improvements

Edge cases to test:

- Role permissions – student cannot access teacher routes
- Lesson status bugs: draft lessons not visible to students
- Review gating: Students cannot get a confirmed score until teacher review
- Retry only allowed once
